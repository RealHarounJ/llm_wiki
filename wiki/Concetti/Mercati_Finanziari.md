# 🏛️ Financial Markets

**Financial Markets** are the infrastructures where financial instruments (stocks, bonds, derivatives) are traded, connecting those who have capital with those who need it.

## Classification

### By Instrument Type
| Market | Instruments | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Stock Market** | Shares (Equity) | NYSE, NASDAQ, Borsa Italiana |
| **Bond Market** | Gov and Corporate bonds | MTS, Euronext |
| **Money Market** | Short-term (<1 year) | T-Bills, commercial paper |
| **Derivatives** | Futures, options, swaps | CME, Eurex |
| **Forex** | Currencies | Decentralized OTC market |

### By Phase
- **Primary Market**: Issuance of new securities (IPOs, private placements).
- **Secondary Market**: Trading of existing securities (stock exchanges).

## Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH)

The **Efficient Market Hypothesis** (Fama, 1970) states that prices incorporate all available information.

| Form | Info in Price | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Weak** | Historical prices | Technical analysis is useless |
| **Semi-strong** | All public info | Fundamental analysis is useless |
| **Strong** | Even private info | Insider trading is useless |

### Market Anomalies
- Momentum effect, January effect, small-cap premium.
- Behavioral finance: cognitive biases of investors.

## Functions of Markets
1. **Price discovery**: determining prices via supply and demand.
2. **Liquidity**: ability to buy/sell quickly.
3. **Risk allocation**: transfer via [[Strumenti_Derivati]].
4. **Capital raising**: financing for firms.

## Connections
- [[Multipli_di_Mercato]] reflect market prices.
- [[CAPM]] assumes efficient markets.
- [[Strumenti_Derivati]] are traded in these markets.
- [[Finanza_Straordinaria]] (IPO, M&A) happens in the primary market.
