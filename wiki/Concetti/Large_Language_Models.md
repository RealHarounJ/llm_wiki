# 🧠 Large Language Models (LLM)

Large Language Models (LLMs) are advanced AI systems capable of understanding and generating human language by utilizing deep neural networks and transformer modeling.

## Overview
LLMs are trained on billions of words from massive datasets. They can perform diverse tasks including:
- **Natural Language Understanding (NLU)**: Comprehending grammar, context, and intent.
- **Generation**: Producing coherent and stylistically consistent text.
- **Synthesization**: Summarization, translation, and classification of content.
- **Conversational Interaction**: Multi-turn dialogues.

## Evolution
1. **Early NLP**: Rule-based and statistical models.
2. **The Transformer Shift**: Introduction of the [[Transformer_Architecture]] in 2017.
3. **Generative Pre-training**: Scaling up parameters and datasets (see [[GPT]]).

## Types of LLM Systems
| Type | Characteristics | Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| **Closed-Book** | Uses pre-trained knowledge only. No live data. | Trivia, general knowledge. |
| **Open-Book** | Retrieves live data from external APIs/databases. | News, live market updates. |
| **Hybrid ([[RAG]])** | Combines internal knowledge with external retrieval. | Enterprise Q&A, technical support. |
