# IO 02: Market Structure and Concentration (Lessons 2 & 3)

## Market Definition
- **NACE / NAICS**: Official statistical classifications based on production processes.
- **Problem**: Standard codes may group non-substitutes together or separate close substitutes. True market definition relies on **demand substitutability** (cross-price elasticity).

## Measuring Concentration
- **Concentration Ratio ($CR_k$)**: Sum of market shares of the top $k$ firms (typically $CR_4$). Ignores the distribution of shares among those firms.
- **Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI)**: Sum of the squared market shares of all firms ($\sum s_i^2$). Captures both firm number and inequality. Used heavily in antitrust/M&A reviews.

## Market Power and Performance
- **Lerner Index (LI)**: Measures market power directly via price markup: $LI = \frac{P - MC}{P}$. In monopoly, $LI = 1/|\epsilon|$. In oligopoly, weighted average: $\sum s_i \frac{P - MC_i}{P}$.

## Determinants of Market Structure
1. **Economies of Scale**: Measured by Scale Economy Index $S = AC/MC$. If $S > 1$, economies exist. Large Minimum Efficient Scale (MES) relative to market demand leads to concentrated markets.
2. **Sunk Costs**: Irreversible investments (non-redeployable indivisibilities) act as a barrier to entry, lowering the number of firms and increasing concentration.
3. **Legal Barriers**: Patents, licenses, monopolies granted by government.
4. **Strategic Behavior**: Limit pricing (pricing low to deter entry), predatory pricing (pricing low to drive out rivals), and capacity expansion (credible commitment to high output).
5. **Horizontal Mergers**: Combinations of competitors (vs Vertical Mergers between supply chain stages).
