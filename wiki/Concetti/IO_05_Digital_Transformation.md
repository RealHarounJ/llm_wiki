# IO 05: Digital Transformation and Internal Effects (Lessons 7 & 9)

## Digital Transformation Impacts
DT is driven by ICT, Data/Connectivity, and AI. It impacts:
- Market Structures (shifts from horizontal/vertical integration).
- Labor Markets (polarization, displacement vs new skills).
- Institutions (laws and privacy).

## Internal Effects on Firms
Digital tech fundamentally changes a firm from the inside *before* external spillovers happen.
1. **Productivity Effects**:
   - *Automation*: Physical (Robots) and Cognitive (Algorithms/AI). Lowers costs and human error.
   - *Efficiency Improvements*: ERPs, real-time tracking, resource optimization.
2. **Organizational Transformation**:
   - *Flattening*: Direct communication reduces middle management layers.
   - *Decentralization*: Frontline employees have access to real-time data to make decisions.
3. **Data-Driven Decision Making**:
   - Shifting from intuition to analytics. Data is a strategic resource (e.g., Zara's demand forecasting).
4. **Innovation Capabilities**: Faster and cheaper experimentation.

## Heterogeneity and Challenges
- Not all firms benefit equally; managerial quality and workforce skills are strictly required (complementary capabilities).
- **Challenges**: High implementation costs, employee resistance, the skills gap, and cybersecurity risks.
