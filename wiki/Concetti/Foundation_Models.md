# 🧠 Foundation Models

A **Foundation Model** is an AI model trained on broad data at scale such that it can be adapted to a wide range of downstream tasks.

## Key Characteristics
- **Scale**: Trained on massive amounts of data (text, images, code).
- **Adaptability**: A single model can be fine-tuned or prompted to perform hundreds of different tasks.
- **Multimodality**: Modern foundation models (e.g., GPT-4o) process and generate text, images, and audio.

## Examples
- **NLP**: GPT series, PaLM, Llama, Claude.
- **Visual**: Stable Diffusion, Midjourney (Visual Foundation Models).

## Emergent Abilities
Large models often display capabilities that are not present in smaller versions, such as multi-step reasoning and in-context learning. These "emergent abilities" appear only after a certain scale of training and compute is reached.
