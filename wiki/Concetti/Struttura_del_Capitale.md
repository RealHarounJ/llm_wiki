# 🏗️ Capital Structure

**Capital Structure** is the mix of **debt** and **equity** a firm uses to finance its operations.

## Components

| Source | Characteristics | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Equity** (Shares) | No repayment obligation, discretionary dividends | More expensive (higher risk for shareholders) |
| **Debt** (Bonds, Loans) | Repayment obligation + interest, priority in liquidation | Less expensive (tax shield + priority) |
| **Hybrids** (Convertibles, Mezzanine) | Mixed characteristics | Intermediate |

## Key Ratios

- **Debt-to-Equity Ratio (D/E)** = Debt / Equity
- **Debt Ratio** = Debt / Total Assets
- **Interest Coverage Ratio** = EBIT / Interest Expense
- **Degree of [[Leva_Finanziaria]]** = measures return amplification

## Optimal Capital Structure Theory

### Trade-Off Theory
There is an **optimal point** where the marginal benefit of the debt tax shield equals the marginal cost of financial distress.
```
Firm Value = V(unlevered) + PV(Tax Shield) - PV(Distress Costs)
```

### Pecking Order Theory (Myers & Majluf)
Firms prefer to finance in this order:
1. **[[Fonti_di_Finanziamento#1. Internal Equity (Equity Interno)|Internal Equity]]** (retained earnings)
2. **[[Fonti_di_Finanziamento#2. External Debt (Debito Esterno)|External Debt]]**
3. **[[Fonti_di_Finanziamento#3. External Equity (Equity Esterno)|External Equity]]** (issuing new shares — last resort)

Motivation: **information asymmetry** between managers and the market.

### [[Teorema_Modigliani_Miller]]
In perfect markets, capital structure is **irrelevant** to firm value. Real-world imperfections (taxes, bankruptcy costs, information asymmetry) make capital structure relevant.

## Connections
- Determines the [[Costo_del_Capitale]] (WACC)
- Linked to [[Leva_Finanziaria]] and [[Rischio_e_Rendimento]]
- Influences [[Politica_dei_Dividendi]] decisions
- [[Corporate_Governance]] determines who decides the structure
