# 💰 Topic 3a — Statement of Cash Flows

Lecture on **March 3, 2026** — Material: Excel text + PDF solution.

## Key Content

### The Cash Flow Statement
Classifies cash flows into three areas:

| Area | Content | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Operating** | Day-to-day operations | Customer receipts, supplier payments, salaries |
| **Investing** | Investments/disinvestments | Purchase/sale of fixed assets, acquisitions |
| **Financing** | Funding and remuneration | Issuing stock/bonds, debt repayment, dividends |

### Indirect Method (most common)
Starts from **Net Income** and adjusts:

```
Operating Cash Flow =
  Net Income
  + Depreciation (non-cash expense)
  + Credit losses
  ± Changes in Working Capital
    - ΔReceivables (↑ A/R = cash absorbed)
    - ΔInventory (↑ stock = cash absorbed)
    + ΔAccounts Payable (↑ A/P = cash generated)
```

### Direct Method
Lists actual receipts and payments:
- Receipts from customers.
- Payments to suppliers.
- Payments for salaries.
- Payments for interest and taxes.

### Interpretation
- **Positive and growing OCF** → healthy business.
- **Negative Investing CF** → firm is investing (good sign if OCF covers it).
- **Financing CF** → depends on the firm's life stage.
