# IO 04: Innovation, R&D and Spillovers (Lessons 6, 8, 10)

## Schumpeterian Hypotheses
1. Concentrated industries (monopolies/oligopolies) are more dynamically efficient (do more R&D).
2. Large firms do more R&D than small firms.

## Types of Innovation
- **Product vs Process**: New goods vs cost-saving methods.
- **Drastic vs Non-Drastic**:
  - *Drastic*: Cost savings so large that the new monopoly price is below the old competitive price ($Q_M > Q_C$).
  - *Non-drastic*: Innovator still constrained by old competitive pricing.

## Incentives to Innovate
- **Replacement Effect (Arrow)**: Monopolists may innovate *less* because new profits destroy existing profits.
- **Efficiency Effect**: Monopolists may innovate *more* to prevent entrants from destroying their monopoly power.

## R&D Spillovers and Cooperation
- **Spillovers ($\beta$)**: Knowledge created by one firm that benefits others. High spillovers reduce incentives for independent R&D (free-riding).
- **Research Joint Ventures (RJV)**: Internalize spillovers ($\beta = 1$), avoiding duplication and increasing total R&D, provided product market competition remains.

## ICT, AI, and the Productivity Paradox
- **GPT (General Purpose Technology)**: Rapid improvement, pervasive application, massive spillovers (Steam, Electricity, ICT, AI).
- **Innovation-enabling technologies**: A subset of GPTs that fundamentally change *how* we invent (e.g., Deep Learning, 3D printing).
- **The Paradox**: Heavy investment in AI but stagnant aggregate productivity.
- **Resolution**: Time lags. Firms require complementary investments in human capital, organizational restructuring, and time to realize TFP gains (similar to the Solow Paradox in the 80s).

## Measuring ICT Spillovers
Spillovers depend on proximity/weights ($W_{ij}$):
1. **Geographical Distance**: Physical closeness (clusters).
2. **Sectoral Distance**: Economic links via Input-Output tables (supply chains).
3. **Technological Distance**: Similarity in patent classes (IPC/CPC) or text embeddings, capturing true absorptive capacity.
