# IO 07: Platform Economics (Lesson 16)

## Definitions
- **Platforms**: Intermediaries that facilitate interactions between users and actively manage network effects.
- **Network Effects**: A user's utility increases/decreases with the network size.

## Types of Network Effects
1. **Within-group (Direct)**: Utility depends on users in the *same* group (e.g., WhatsApp, Waze). Can be positive (social norms) or negative (congestion).
2. **Cross-group (Indirect)**: Utility depends on users in the *other* group (e.g., Amazon buyers & sellers). Creates an "Attraction Spiral."
3. **Pendulum (Attraction/Repulsion)**: Group A likes Group B, but Group B dislikes Group A (e.g., Advertisers and Readers).

## Tipping and Monopolies
- Positive direct network effects create self-reinforcing attraction loops.
- **Winner-Takes-All**: The market tips toward a single dominant platform. Users may coordinate on the popular platform even if it's not their intrinsic preference.
- *Equilibrium*: Unstable equilibrium occurs at 50% (tipping point). Stable equilibria are at 0% or 100%.

## Limits to Winner-Takes-All
Mechanisms that allow multiple platforms to coexist ("Competition IN the market"):
1. **Differentiation**: Users have heterogeneous preferences.
2. **Multihoming**: Users can join multiple platforms simultaneously (e.g., owning multiple social media accounts).
3. **Interoperability**: Users on platform A can interact with users on platform B.
4. **Negative Network Effects**: Overcrowding or congestion (e.g., Airbnb).
