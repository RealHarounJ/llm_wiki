# IO 01: Basic Microeconomic Tools (Lesson 1)

## Perfect Competition vs Monopoly
- **Perfect Competition**: Efficiency benchmark. Firms and consumers are price-takers ($P = MC$). Reallocating resources cannot make someone better off without making another worse off (Pareto efficiency).
- **Monopoly**: A single firm sets the price. The firm maximizes profit where $MR = MC$, resulting in a price higher than marginal cost ($P > MC$) and an output lower than the competitive level ($Q_M < Q_C$).

## Efficiency and Surplus
- **Consumer Surplus**: Difference between maximum willingness to pay and actual price.
- **Producer Surplus**: Difference between price received and marginal cost.
- **Deadweight Loss (DWL)**: The loss of total surplus under monopoly due to underproduction. The monopolist cannot appropriate this DWL unless perfect price discrimination is applied.

## Static vs Dynamic Efficiency
- **Static Efficiency**: Optimal allocation of resources at a given point in time (low prices today, characteristic of perfect competition).
- **Dynamic Efficiency**: Creation of new goods, services, and technologies over time (innovation tomorrow, often requires market power to recoup R&D costs).

## Discounting and Present Value
Firms evaluate long-term investments by discounting future cash flows:
$$ PV = \frac{Z_1}{(1+r)} + \frac{Z_2}{(1+r)^2} + ... + \frac{Z_T}{(1+r)^T} $$
Where $R = 1/(1+r)$ is the discount factor.
