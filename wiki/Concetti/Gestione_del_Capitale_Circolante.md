# 🔄 Working Capital Management

**Working Capital Management** involves the administration of current assets and liabilities to ensure operational liquidity.

## Definition

```
Net Working Capital (NWC) = Current Assets - Current Liabilities
```

### Components

| Current Assets | Current Liabilities |
|---|---|
| Cash & Equivalents | Accounts Payable |
| Accounts Receivable | Short-term Debt |
| Inventory | Accrued Expenses |

## The Cash Cycle

```
Cash Cycle = Inventory Days + Receivable Days - Payable Days
```

| Component | Formula | Objective |
|---|---|---|
| **Inventory Days** | (Inventory / COGS) × 365 | Minimize |
| **Receivable Days** | (A/R / Revenue) × 365 | Minimize |
| **Payable Days** | (A/P / Purchases) × 365 | Maximize |

**Objective**: Reduce the cash cycle → less capital tied up → more [[Free_Cash_Flow]].

## Management of Components

### Accounts Receivable
- Credit policy: who, how much, how long.
- Trade-off: more credit → more sales vs. higher default risk.
- Tools: factoring, credit insurance.

### Inventory
- Trade-off: high stock (safety) vs. low stock (efficiency).
- Models: EOQ (Economic Order Quantity), JIT (Just in Time).

### Cash
- Motives for holding cash: transactional, precautionary, speculative.
- Trade-off: liquidity vs. return (opportunity cost).

## Connections
- Directly impacts [[Free_Cash_Flow]] (ΔWC).
- Linked to [[Politica_dei_Dividendi]] (distribution capacity).
- Influences operating [[Rischio_e_Rendimento]].
- Relevant for [[Capital_Budgeting]] (incremental CF estimation).
