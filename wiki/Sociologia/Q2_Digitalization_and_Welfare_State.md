# Q2: The Effects of Digitalization on Welfare State Dynamics

## 1. Three Fundamental Dilemmas (Future of Democratic Welfare State)

### Dilemma 1: The End of the "Veil of Ignorance"
- Traditional social insurance works because **no one knows who will get sick** → collective solidarity.
- Digitalization allows **individual risk profiling** → "lifts the veil" → destroys the incentive for collective insurance.

### Dilemma 2: [[ADM Automated Decision-Making]] Systems
- Governments use AI algorithms to manage welfare efficiently.
- **Risk:** Algorithms can be **opaque and discriminatory**.
- **The SyRI Case (Netherlands):** Fraud-detection algorithm caused *systematic ethnic discrimination* → 26,000 families forced to repay benefits unfairly → Dutch government resigned 2021.

### Dilemma 3: Blurred Employment Boundaries
- The line between self-employed and employed disappears (gig workers).
- Traditional welfare systems based on stable contracts fail to protect **atypical workers**.
- See also: [[Platform Work]], [[Q1_Digitalization_and_Labour_Market]]

## 2. [[Pension Systems Three Pillars]] — Chapter 15

| Pillar | Type | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **First** | Public | Mandatory state pensions, funded by social contributions (PAYG) |
| **Second** | Private Occupational | Company/sector pension funds via collective agreements |
| **Third** | Private Personal | Individual voluntary pension plans |

### Two Main European Models:
| Feature | Social Insurance (Italy, Spain) | Multi-Pillar (UK, Netherlands) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Funding** | Pay-as-you-go (PAYG) | Mixed (state + private markets) |
| **Calculation** | Earnings-related | Flat-rate + market returns |

### Three Scenarios for the Future of Work:
1. **Job Destruction:** Robots replace humans → contributions collapse → structural pension threat. *Possible fix: "Robot Tax".*
2. **Job Creation:** Technology creates new roles → no additional risk → "active ageing" possible.
3. **Job Transformation (Most Likely):** Middle-skill jobs replaced (routinization) → precarious work → inadequate pensions.

### Political Consequences for Social Partners:
- **Traditional power:** Unions governed pensions because funding came from *their* contributions.
- **Digital disruption:** If funding shifts to general taxation (Robot Tax), unions **lose governance power**.
- **Membership decline:** Gig workers are hard to organize → unions lose political legitimacy.

## 3. New Policy Paradigms

### [[Social Investment State]] (Dominant Approach)
- Invest in **human capital**: education, vocational training, lifelong learning.
- **Criticism:** Benefits high-skilled urban areas; deepens divide with declining rural regions.

### [[Universal Basic Income]] (Radical Approach)
- Current welfare fails gig workers: insufficient contributions, access thresholds not met.
- **UBI logic:** Social protection based on **citizenship**, not employment status.
- **Challenge:** "Ethos of creativity" — digitalization glorifies creativity, stigmatizing "losers" as deserving poverty → makes UBI politically difficult.

## 4. Changing Political Landscape

| Actor | Before | After Digitalization |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Trade Unions** | Strong — stable manufacturing workers | Weakened — cannot organize platform workers |
| **Employer Orgs** | Key welfare governance partners | Reduced legitimacy |
| **Global Tech Companies** | Marginal | **New dominant political actors** |
| **"Losers" of digitalization** | Middle class, social-democratic voters | Demand short-term compensation → fuel populism |

## Exam Summary Sentence
*"Digitalization challenges welfare states through three dilemmas: the end of collective solidarity (risk profiling), discriminatory ADM systems (SyRI case), and failure of traditional welfare to protect gig workers. Pension systems face existential threats in a 'Job Destruction' scenario, marginalizing trade unions. The response involves Social Investment, Universalism, and UBI — though each faces significant political and economic obstacles."*
