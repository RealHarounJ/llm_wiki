Introduction
Image of Seoul, South Korea
South Korea stands as a global technological powerhouse where digitalization serves as the core of modern governance.   Our research explores how the nation’s strategy integrates high-speed connectivity into both the social safety net and the labor market.   While the welfare perspective focuses on data-driven, proactive support for vulnerable populations, the labor perspective addresses the structural transition toward AI-integrated industries.

=== SLIDE 16 ===
Policy scenario in South Korea
To ensure trustworthy adoption, policies must protect worker health and privacy by updating safety regulations and strengthening data protection frameworks against surveillance.
AI-driven decisions require human oversight and transparency, establishing accountability mechanisms, such as mandatory audits and impact assessments, is crucial for maintaining a responsible and ethically managed digital workplace.  South Korea has introduced a comprehensive regulatory framework to manage the risks of artificial intelligence, most notably through the AI Basic Act (2025), which will be implemented in 2026. This law aims to promote innovation while ensuring the safe and trustworthy use of AI.
Image of the South Korean parliament, Seoul

=== SLIDE 17 ===
Political scenario in South Korea
Political scenario of digitalization in South Korea is characterised by a strong role of the government and
large corporations
, alongside relatively weaker worker representation.
A
